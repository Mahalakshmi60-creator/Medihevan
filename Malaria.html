MALARIA

What is Malaria?

Malaria is a life-threatening disease caused by Plasmodium parasites, which are transmitted to humans through the bites of infected female Anopheles mosquitoes. Malaria is prevalent in tropical and subtropical regions, particularly in Africa, Asia, and South America.

Transmission:

Malaria spreads through the bite of an infected female Anopheles mosquito. It can also spread via:
1.Blood transfusion
2.Sharing needles or syringes
3.From mother to child during pregnancy

1.High fever
2.Chills and sweating

6.Fatigue
In severe cases: organ failure, seizures, or death

Diagnosis:

1.Microscopic examination of blood smears
2.Rapid diagnostics test(RDTs)

Prevention:

1.Using insecticide-treated bed nets
2.Indoor residue spraying
3.Preventive medications
4.Eliminating mosquito breeding sites



Malaria is preventable and curable with proper measures, making awareness, prompt treatment, and prevention crucial to combat the disease.
