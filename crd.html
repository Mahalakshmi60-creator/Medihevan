RESPIRATORY DISEASES

What is Chronic respiratory diseases?

Chronic Respiratory Diseases (CRDs) are long-term diseases of the airways and other structures of the lungs. They are characterized by persistent breathing problems and airflow limitation, which can significantly impact a person's quality of life. These diseases are not fully curable but can often be managed to reduce symptoms and improve function.

Types of Chronic respiratory diseases?

1.Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
2.Asthma
3.Pulmonary Hypertension
4.Interstitial Lung Diseases
5.Occupational Lung Diseases
6.Cystic Fibrosis

Symptoms:

1.Chronic cough
2.Shortness of breath
3.Wheezing
4.Chest tightness
5.Excessive mucus production
6.Frequent respiratory
7.Fatigue

Diagnosis:

1.Pulmonary Function Tests (PFTs): Assess lung function
2.Chest X-rays or CT Scans: Detect structural changes in the lungs
3.Blood Tests: Check oxygen levels or genetic markers
4.Allergy Tests: Identify triggers for asthma
5.Sputum Tests: Identify infections or inflammatory markers

Treatment:

1.Lifestyle changes
2.Medications
3.Oxygen therapy
4.Pulmonary Rehabilitation
5.Surgery

Prevention:

1.Avoid smoking and exposure to air pollutants.
2.Use protective equipment in workplaces with hazardous materials.
3.Ensure proper ventilation in living and working environments.
4.Get vaccinated against respiratory infections like influenza and pneumonia.
5.Manage existing conditions like asthma to prevent complications.



Early diagnosis, appropriate treatment, and lifestyle modifications are essential to managing CRDs and improving the quality of life.
