TUBERCULOSIS

What is tuberculosis?

Tuberculosis (TB) is a contagious disease caused by the bacterium Mycobacterium tuberculosis. It primarily affects the lungs but can also impact other parts of the body such as the brain, spine, or kidneys. TB is one of the leading causes of infectious disease-related deaths globally, especially in developing countries.

Transmission:

TB spreads through the air when an infected person coughs, sneezes, or talks. Prolonged exposure to an infected person increases the risk of transmission.

Types of TB:

Latent TB: The bacteria are present in the body but inactive, causing no symptoms and not being contagious.
Active TB: The bacteria are active, causing symptoms and making the person contagious.

Symptoms(for pulmonary TB):

1.Persistent cough lasting 3 weeks or longer
2.Coughing up blood or mucus
3.Chest pain
4.Fever and chills
5.Night sweats
6.Fatigue
7.Unintentional weight loss

Diagnosis:

1.Blood test
2.Chest X-ray
3.Skin test

Prevention:

1.Vaccination
2.Early diagnosis and treatment
3.Proper ventilation and avoiding close contact with infected individuals



If left untreated, TB can be fatal, underscoring the importance of timely diagnosis and treatment
